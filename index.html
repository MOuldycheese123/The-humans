The Crusades

The Crusades were a series of interfaith conflicts between Christians and Muslims that were largely waged to seize control of crucial locations revered by both faiths. Between 1096 and 1291, eight significant Crusade expeditions of different size, power, and degree of success took place. The expensive, bloody, and frequently brutal wars raised the stature of European Christians and made them significant players in the Middle Eastern land wars.
